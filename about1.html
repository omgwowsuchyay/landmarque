Using our proprietary investment process, worldwide network and unique knowledge base, we invest alongside our clients to benefit from the key structural changes in our world today.

01.

Idea generation

Munro identifies sustainable growth trends that are under-appreciated, not well understood and mispriced by the market, and in our view, the resulting winning and losing stocks.

02.

Idea evaluation (qualitative)

Munro looks for five key company characteristics to gauge whether a listed company is likely to benefit or lose from a particular growth trend and whether that will be sustained over an extended period of time.

03.

Idea evaluation (quantitative)

Munro uses three valuation based tests to quantify earnings upside/downside, multiple upside / downside and time based catalysts. Ideas graduate from the universe to the portfolio based on the relative upside generated from these tests.

04.

Portfolio construction

Qualitative and quantitative tests combine to build a high conviction, index, region and sector unaware portfolio of investments. Munro's absolute return  heritage means risk management and proprietary stop loss review framework are integral.

Finding long-term winners

Earnings growth drives stock prices

Companies that consistently earn more than the year before are generally rewarded with higher stock prices over time.

Sustained earnings growth

Consistent growth, independent of cyclical factors and above the peer group, is valued at higher multiples.

Identify mispriced growth

Consensus earnings estimates often underestimate growth,  its sustainability and cash generation capacity, allowing opportunities to invest in stocks well below their intrinsic value.

Jennifer Burnside

Jennifer Burnside

Drawing on nearly three decades of industry experience, Jennifer serves as MA’s Director of Documentation and Construction Administration. In this leadership role, she is responsible for developing and implementing processes that improve MA’s documentation standards, protocols during construction, and the overall quality of the firm’s project deliverables. Monthly, she facilitates cross-studio communication with the firm’s project managers and project architects to proactively discuss new ideas and practical, implementable solutions to meet the firm’s high standards for quality control, project efficiency, and client satisfaction. Since joining the firm, Jennifer has also spearheaded construction administration services on several of the firm’s large-scale developments in Philadelphia and Washington, D.C.

Bachelor of Architecture,

Angela Caviezel

Angela Caviezel, AIA, LEED GA

As Director of Interiors, Angela oversees the delivery of all interior design services—working closely with the firm’s architectural studios to ensure each project reflects a holistic and integrated approach. Her extensive experience includes designing and executing a broad range of residential, commercial, hospitality, and mixed-use projects within the U.S. and internationally. Most recently, Angela has overseen the design of unit interiors and amenity spaces within several multifamily projects, most significantly including Front & York—a 1.2 million-square-foot mixed-use multifamily development in Dumbo, Brooklyn, that encompasses more than 720 residential units and 150,000 square feet of amenity space.

A proven leader, Angela is known for her ability to nurture constructive relationships between clients, consultant teams, and stakeholders to advance complex projects.

Tom Lewis

Tom Lewis, AIA, LEED AP BD+C

Tom is a Studio Director at MA recognized for his ability to deliver a wide range of complex residential, commercial, and institutional projects, including those involving highly publicized entitlement processes. This has recently included the delivery of Atlantic Plumbing 901W—a unique 354,000-square-foot mixed-use multifamily development in Washington, D.C., consisting of two 11-story through-block structures joined by a sky-bridge. He is currently overseeing several large-scale mixed-use projects in various stages of completion across the U.S., including the delivery of 350 & 400 Summer Street—two adjacent commercial towers in Boston's Seaport District totaling more than 1 million square feet, as well as the documentation of a 380,000-square-foot dormitory project MA designed for the U.C. College System. 

In addition, Tom leads MA's Sustainability Committee, ensuring the firm continues to employ the latest advancements in green technology and design.

Ian Mills

Ian Mills, RA

Based in MA’s New Orleans Office, Studio Director Ian Mills oversees many of the firm’s projects in the south and on the west coast, including a wide range of hotels and multifamily projects that offer unique hospitality experiences. Recently, this has included overseeing several projects within the master-planned Water Street Tampa development: The EDITION Hotel & Residence, which features a blend of condominium units and hotel rooms, as well as 1050 Water Street, a 788,000-square-foot development featuring 500 residential units—a portion of which will be furnished and operated under an extended-stay hotel brand. Both projects are currently in construction. Also in Florida, Ian was responsible for the delivery of the recently completed Goodtime Hotel within the Miami Beach Art Deco Historic District. Back in New Orleans, he is currently overseeing the adaptive reuse of a historic building as a 96,000-square-foot boutique hotel with 50 apartment-style guest suites, a food hall, cafe, and multiple bar venues.

Lucas Posada

Lucas Posada, AIA, LEED AP

As a Studio Director at MA, Lucas leads a team of architects and designers in the delivery of a wide range of projects varying in typology, scale, and complexity. This has recently included overseeing the delivery of 520 West 20th—an 85,000-square-foot adaptive reuse project in New York City involving the transformation of a historic warehouse into a contemporary work environment inspired by art and technology.

And while Lucas has a special appreciation for working on projects that shape the New York City neighborhoods he frequents, he also enjoys the challenge of designing buildings outside of the city, particularly those within historic, post-industrial districts. His team is currently leading several large-scale efforts throughout the U.S., including 900 Randolph—a new 43-story multifamily tower within Chicago’s historic Fulton Market District and The Wharf Parcel 10—a sustainability-focused office building in Washington, D.C.

Becca Roderick

Becca Roderick

As MA’s Design Director of Interiors, Becca works closely with the firm’s Principal, Design Director of Architecture, and Studio Directors to steer the development of warm yet refined spaces that harmoniously integrate within a project’s architectural and commercial contexts. In this leadership role, she helps to establish overall design goals and standards for the firm, inspire creativity, and ensure that projects are completed to the highest level of quality.

Leveraging more than 15 years of industry experience, Becca’s award-winning work is guided by the principle that the most successful spaces don’t just look beautiful, they feel good as well. Prior to joining MA, she served as the Director of Interior Design at One Kings Lane and formerly AvroKO, where she received recognition for highly-publicized projects, such as The Zodiac Room restaurant at Neiman Marcus in Hudson Yards, which was featured in Interior Design and Hospitality Design magazines; the Perry Lane Hotel, named the "Best New Hotel in the World" by Travel + Leisure; as well as the renovation of Minnie Driver's Malibu residence, featured in House Beautiful and People.

Lyle Starr

Lyle Starr

As the Director of MA Art Services, Lyle leads the design and development of all interior and exterior art programming for the firm's projects. Specializing in the thoughtful curation of artwork and the coordination of original commissions, he has recently overseen the selection and placement of art at Nexus Club New York, a 34,000-square-foot social club offering a wide range of amenity spaces and services in Manhattan. Lyle has also conceived and executed several conceptual, art-based placemaking initiatives such as the neon “WELCOME” sign that illuminates the New York City skyline.
